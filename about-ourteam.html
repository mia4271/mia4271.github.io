Canadian Team



YuenPao Woo

Senator

Senator Woo has over three decades’ experience in strategy and policy for business, government and not-for- profit organizations. Widely recognized as a leading thinker on international economic issues and Canada-Asia relations, he was appointed to the Senate of Canada in November 2016, and sits as an independent representing British Columbia. Prior to joining the Senate, he was President of HQ Vancouver, a public-private partnership that promotes British Columbia as a location for head offices of international companies. From 2005-2014, he was President and CEO of the Asia Pacific Foundation of Canada, where he continues to serve as Distinguished East Asia Fellow. Senator Woo is a member of the Trilateral Commission; Senior Resident Fellow at the Beedie School of Business, Simon Fraser University; Senior Fellow in Public Policy at the Institute of Asian Research, University of British Columbia; as well as co-founder and President of China Global: The Vancouver Society for Promotion of Chinese Art and Culture. He is chair of the board of the Vancouver Academy of Music, and a member of the Global Council of the Asia Society, as well as on the Advisory Boards of the Mosaic Institute and the Canadian Ditchley Foundation. Senator Woo is a active proponent of closer ties between Canada and Asian countries, and has led various initiatives that promote dialogue and collaboration on social policy issues such as aging and seniors care, women in the work force, and immigration.

Colin Hansen

President and Chief Executive Officer with AdvantageBC

Colin Hansen is the President and Chief Executive Officer with AdvantageBC. In 1996, he was elected to the British Columbia Legislature and served as Deputy Premier and Minister of Finance from June 2008 to March 2011. He also served for almost four years as Minister of Health and three years as Minister of Economic Development, which included responsibility for the 2010 Olympics and the Asia Pacific Initiative. He serves on several not-for-profit boards including Arthritis Research Canada, the Fraser Basin Council, Jack Austin School of Asia Pacific Studies at Simon Fraser University and the China Council at the University of British Columbia. In addition, he is past chair of the Board of Directors for the Transportation Investment Corporation (a provincial crown corporation). Before politics, he was President and Founding Partner of Image Group, BC’s largest promotional products company, and the Vice-President, Finance and Administration for the Asia Pacific Foundation of Canada.

Chinese Team



XiaoLi Feng

President of China Association of Social Welfare and Senior Service Chief Consular of Comfort Maple Aging Innovations Inc.

Feng Xiaoli, graduated from Northwest Normal University; From 1982 to 1995, worked as Director in Gansu Provincial Government General Office and The Central China and Japan Youth Exchange Center; From October 1998 to February 2014, she served as Deputy Director and Director of the Social Welfare Center of the Ministry of Civil Affairs successively; From March 2014 to March 2016, worked as the inspector of the Department of Social Welfare and Charity Promotion of the Ministry of Civil Affairs; From 2010 to 2016, she serves as the Secretary General and Vice President of China Association of Social Welfare and Senior Service; Since May 2016, she has been the President of China Association of Social Welfare and Senior Service; She is also a member of the National Committee of Social Welfare Service Technology Standardization Working Committee, member of the Expert Committee of the Ministry of Civil Affairs, part-time researcher of Zhejiang University, guest professor of Hangzhou Normal University and honorary dean of the School of Welfare of Beijing College of Social Management. She has made great achievements in carrying out senior service policy research, organizing the researches of senior service standardization, informatics technology, service techniques, professional training and developing senior care teaching materials; initiated and completed Study on Senior Service Industry Standard System Framework, National Information System for Aged Services, the Applied Scientific Research Projects on the Development of Social Senior Service System-Research of Ten Service Management Model, Practical Training Materials for Senior Service Personnel and applied across China, having a good impact in senior service industry. From 2012 to 2018, she has planned and organized the China International Senior Services Expo (CISSE) for 7 consecutive years with the government departments and act as Director of CISSE organizing committee.
